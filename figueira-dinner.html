The Restaurant

A dining room built around a living giant

The restaurant takes its name — and its soul — from the century-old fig tree that rises straight through the dining room, its canopy spreading under a glass roof. Tables wrap around the trunk, so every seat feels like dinner in a lit-up garden. It's one of São Paulo's landmark rooms, a Michelin Guide selection, and the flagship experience of the Rubaiyat family.

Farm to table, literally. The Rubaiyat group pioneered farm-to-table in Brazil: the beef comes from their own Rubaiyat Farm herds, which earned a spot in the World's Best Steak Restaurants guide. The glass-walled kitchen sends out wood-fired grills with Mediterranean touches — watch the brigade work the flames while you wait for the couvert.
What to Order

The family game plan

Portions are generous and it's all shared, family-style — don't fill up on the (legendary) couvert. Two prime cuts to carve for the table, a bright seafood starter, the Thomas salad and local sides passed around, dessert to finish. Rough guide: ~R$1,135 in food for the four of us (about US$205), ~R$1,545 with drinks + dessert + serviço. Note: the famous feijoada buffet is Saturday lunch only, so it's not on for our 7 PM dinner — we'll catch it another day.

The couvert

Everyone · to start

Their famous opening spread: warm pão de queijo and focaccia, charcuterie, olives, grilled vegetables and fresh mozzarella. It's charged per person (modest — think appetizer money, not entrée money), so take it for all four and treat it as the first course. Declining is also perfectly normal in Brazil, but you won't want to.

Grilled Pitu Shrimp + tartare to share

Everyone · starters

Our seafood pick: grilled pitu shrimp with pistachio butter (R$159) — pitu is a Brazilian freshwater prawn, so it's local and a great shared starter. Alongside it, the hand-cut steak tartare with mustard ice cream & potato chips (R$98). (Photo is representative.)

Picanha + Baby Beef

The main event · shared

The two most local, most-praised cuts — order both and carve across the table. Picanha strip steak (R$236) is the iconic Brazilian cut; Baby Beef / ribeye (R$242) is the single most-loved dish in reviews, from Rubaiyat's own farm. Get chimichurri on the side (R$18).

Thomas Salad + local sides

Shared · Jane's favorites

The Thomas Salad (R$98) — arugula, Iberian ham, caramelized figs, walnuts, gorgonzola — the most interesting salad, and plenty of greens for Jane. To round out the table: creamy pupunha palm heart au gratin (R$71) and Luiz Tavares farofa (R$49) — both local and veg-forward.

Mil-folhas & drinks

Everyone

Save room: the mil-folhas (their Napoleon) is the dessert to share — one or two for the table. Drinks: a caipirinha each for Evan & Jane (lime or passion-fruit), a virgin caipirinha for Ian, and água de coco (fresh coconut water) for Zach.

If You Change Your Mind

Alternatives & swaps

Easy substitutions at the table — same spirit, different pick.

Steak — showpiece🥩
Swap the two cuts for Queen Beef (R$478, serves 2) for one dramatic shared steak, or go big with the Master Beef / Tomahawk (R$425). Same per-person cost as picanha + baby beef, more theater.
Steak — other cuts🔥
Prefer variety? Bife de chorizo / Sirloin Entrecote (R$211) with soufflé potatoes is the house classic; Denver Steak (R$292) and Skirt Steak (R$198) are both excellent off the wood grill.
Seafood — most local🐟
Add or swap in the Catch of the Day with moqueca sauce (R$184) — a Bahian coconut-and-palm-oil stew with plantain and farofa. The most authentically Brazilian seafood on the menu.
Starters🍽️
In place of tartare: Roasted Pupunha Palm Heart (clay oven · R$80), Tenderloin Carpaccio (R$86), or Scallop Carpaccio with citric sauce (R$116).
Salad for Jane🥗
Lighter than the Thomas: Baby Romaine with palm heart, brie & almond crisp (R$91), the Rubaiyat Salad (R$71), or the vegan Palm Heart & Watercress (R$76).
Sides🍠
Mix in roasted purple sweet potato (R$46), roasted vegetables (R$63), or Biro-Biro rice (R$58) if you want more on the table.

Know Before You Go

Practical bits

  • Getting there: it's on the same street grid as the hotel — walk Alameda Santos → Rua Haddock Lobo, ~15 minutes through Jardins, or a 5-minute Uber. Well-lit, busy area in the evening.
  • The reservation is under Mrs. Jane Lew, 7:00 PM, party of 4. Tables are held 10 minutes, then it's first-come — aim to leave the hotel by 6:40.
  • Paying: dinner is paid directly to the restaurant (not through Jacada). Cards widely accepted. The 10% serviço is typically on the bill — no extra tip expected.
  • Dress: smart casual — collared shirt territory, no need for jackets.
  • Phone: +55 (11) 3087-1399 if plans change.
  • Jet-lag insurance: dinner at 7 is early by paulistano standards — the room will fill as you finish. Perfect for a 9:30 AM lobby pickup tomorrow.
