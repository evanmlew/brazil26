Day 02

Bom dia, São Paulo

São Paulo · Jardins
  • 9:00 AM — Land at Guarulhos; our driver meets us just past customs with a Jacada sign
  • Early check-in at the Renaissance São Paulo (Alameda Santos, Jardins) — showers, naps, and a slow afternoon
  • Walkable from the hotel: Avenida Paulista is 2 minutes away — on Sundays it closes to cars and fills with musicians and street food

  • Rua Oscar Freire (~10 min) — Brazil's most stylish shopping street: the Havaianas flagship, Brazilian designers, and excellent gelato

Dinner · 7:00 PM

Figueira Rubaiyat (Rua Haddock Lobo — a short walk from the hotel) — São Paulo's famous grill, built around a magnificent century-old Bengal fig tree; one Tripadvisor reviewer calls it "a dining experience like no other." The jaguar quest 🐆

Onçafari Jaguar Project
  • Full day & evening embedded with the Onçafari jaguar habituation team (exact day confirmed at the lodge)
  • Base-station briefing, then fieldwork: camera traps, tracks and prints, VHF collar telemetry — maybe tracking on foot with the experts
  • If the team pins a location by day, we head out at sunset or after dinner for the real thing: a wild jaguar in the dark
🐾
Dinner

At the lodge — possibly interrupted by a jaguar. We're okay with that.

🐆 FUN FACT — Onçafari was founded in 2011 by Mário Haberfeld, a Brazilian ex-Formula 1 test driver who traded racing for rewilding — it was the first project in the world to return captive-born jaguars to the wild. Some 60–80 jaguars roam the refuge, and in recent seasons nearly every guest staying 3–4 days has seen one. Our odds are good.

Dinner · 9:30 PM

Marine Restô, the Fairmont's 6th-floor restaurant — a Michelin Guide selection ranked in Tripadvisor's top 25 of nearly 6,000 Rio restaurants. Everything comes off two Josper charcoal ovens; reviewers rave about the grilled pirarucu (the Amazon's giant fish, for sharing), the shrimp moqueca risotto, and the picanha. Note: the terrace is closed for facade work this month — we dine inside, same ocean view. If flights run late, room service with a view.

🛬 FUN FACT — Santos Dumont airport is named for Alberto Santos-Dumont, the dapper Brazilian aviator who made the first public airplane flight in Europe in 1906 — Brazilians will happily tell you the Wright brothers came second.

Dinner · 7:30 PM

Zazá Bistrô Tropical, Ipanema — 25 years of playful Brazilian-Asian cooking in a candlelit century-old mansion (ask for the cushion-floor tables upstairs). Reviewers' picks: the Amazonian gyoza with tucupi, squid-and-fish moqueca with passion-fruit farofa for Jane & Zach, seared tuna in lemon-zest crust, and the organic chicken curry.

🎨 FUN FACT — Selarón's 215 steps are tiled with pieces from more than 60 countries — the Chilean artist called it "my tribute to the Brazilian people" and kept adding tiles until his death. And the Sugarloaf cable car has been running since 1912 — it was the first in Brazil.
